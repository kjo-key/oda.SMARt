Chemical Bonding: Types and Formulas

1. Ionic Bonding

Ionic bonding occurs when atoms transfer electrons to form oppositely charged ions that attract each other.

  • Electrostatic Attraction: The force that holds oppositely charged ions together.
  • Formation of Ions: Metals lose electrons to form cations, while non-metals gain electrons to form anions.
  • Octet Rule: Atoms tend to gain, lose, or share electrons to achieve a full outer shell (usually 8 electrons).

Example: Sodium (Na) and Chlorine (Cl) form Sodium Chloride (NaCl) by ionic bonding.


            Na+ + Cl- → NaCl

2. Covalent Bonding

Covalent bonding occurs when atoms share one or more pairs of electrons to achieve a stable electron configuration.

  • Electron Sharing: Atoms share electrons to complete their outer shell.
  • Types of Bonds: Single bond (one pair), double bond (two pairs), and triple bond (three pairs).
  • Polar vs Nonpolar Bonds: Polar covalent bonds have unequal sharing, while nonpolar bonds share electrons equally.

Example: In a water molecule (H₂O), oxygen shares electrons with hydrogen to form covalent bonds.


            H2 + O → H2O

3. Metallic Bonding

Metallic bonding occurs when metal atoms share electrons freely in a "sea of electrons."

  • Electron Sea Model: Electrons are delocalized and move freely, which gives metals their properties.
  • Properties: Malleable, ductile, conductive of electricity and heat.

4. Coordinate Covalent Bonding

Coordinate covalent bonding happens when both electrons in a shared pair come from the same atom.

  • Electron Donation: One atom donates both electrons in the bond.
  • Example: Ammonium ion (NH₄⁺) formation.

            NH3 + H+ → NH4+
