Biochemical Molecules

Introduction to Biochemical Molecules

Biochemical molecules are the basic building blocks of life. They include various compounds found in living organisms that are involved in the essential biochemical processes that sustain life. These molecules can be divided into four major groups: carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, and nucleic acids. Each of these groups plays a crucial role in the structure and function of cells.

1. Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are organic compounds made up of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. They serve as a primary source of energy for organisms and are also involved in the structure of cells. Carbohydrates can be classified into:

  • Monosaccharides: Simple sugars like glucose, fructose, and galactose.
  • Disaccharides: Two monosaccharides linked together, like sucrose (glucose + fructose) and lactose (glucose + galactose).
  • Polysaccharides: Long chains of monosaccharides, such as starch, glycogen, and cellulose.

Glucose Metabolism Formula (Cellular Respiration):

C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + 38ATP

Where:

  • C6H12O6 is glucose
  • O2 is oxygen
  • CO2 is carbon dioxide
  • H2O is water
  • ATP is adenosine triphosphate, the energy currency of the cell.

Polysaccharide Structure:

  • Starch (C6H10O5)n
  • Glycogen (C6H10O5)n

2. Proteins

Proteins are made up of amino acids linked together by peptide bonds. Proteins are essential for various functions in the body, including catalysis of biochemical reactions (enzymes), structural components of cells, immune responses, and transportation of molecules.

Amino Acid Structure: Each amino acid has a basic structure with an amino group (−NH2), a carboxyl group (−COOH), a hydrogen atom (−H), and a variable side chain (R group).

Protein Synthesis (Translation): Proteins are synthesized in cells through translation, where messenger RNA (mRNA) is translated into a specific sequence of amino acids.

mRNA → Amino Acids → Protein

Enzyme Kinetics (Michaelis-Menten Equation):

V = (Vmax [S]) / (Km + [S])

Where:

  • V is the reaction rate
  • Vmax is the maximum reaction rate
  • [S] is the concentration of the substrate
  • Km is the Michaelis constant.

3. Lipids

Lipids are hydrophobic or amphipathic molecules that include fats, oils, and phospholipids. They are primarily used for energy storage, forming cellular membranes, and signaling molecules.

Triglycerides: Composed of three fatty acids and one glycerol molecule. They are the main form of energy storage in animals.

Triglyceride = Glycerol + 3 × Fatty Acids

Phospholipids: These are the main component of cell membranes. They consist of two fatty acid chains, a glycerol backbone, and a phosphate group.

Phospholipid Bilayer (Membrane Structure): The hydrophilic heads face outward, and the hydrophobic tails face inward, creating a semipermeable membrane.

4. Nucleic Acids

Nucleic acids are the molecules that carry genetic information. There are two major types of nucleic acids: DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) and RNA (Ribonucleic Acid). DNA is responsible for storing and transmitting genetic information, while RNA plays a role in protein synthesis.

DNA Structure: Composed of four nucleotide bases: adenine (A), thymine (T), cytosine (C), and guanine (G). The structure is a double helix, with two strands connected by base pairs (A-T, C-G).

RNA Structure: Similar to DNA but single-stranded, with uracil (U) replacing thymine (T).

DNA Replication: The process where DNA is copied before cell division. The formula can be summarized as:

DNA → DNA Polymerase → New DNA Strand

Transcription (DNA to mRNA): The process of making an RNA copy of a gene's DNA sequence.

DNA → RNA Polymerase → mRNA

Translation (mRNA to Protein): The process where the mRNA is translated into a sequence of amino acids.

mRNA → Amino Acids → Protein

Key Formulas:

  • Glycolysis (Glucose to Pyruvate):
    • C6H12O6 → 2C3H4O3 + 2ATP
  • Citric Acid Cycle (Krebs Cycle):
    • 2C3H4O3 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + 36ATP
  • ATP Hydrolysis:
    • ATP + H2O → ADP + Pi + Energy
  • Nucleotides to Nucleic Acids:
    • Nucleotide → Phosphodiester Bond → Polynucleotide Chain
  • Michaelis-Menten Enzyme Kinetics:
    • V = (Vmax [S]) / (Km + [S])
  • Membrane Transport (Fick's Law):
    • J = -D · (dC/dx)
