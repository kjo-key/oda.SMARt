Human Biology

Human Anatomy and Physiology

The Circulatory System

The circulatory system is responsible for transporting blood throughout the body. It consists of the heart, blood vessels, and blood. It delivers oxygen and nutrients to cells and removes metabolic waste like carbon dioxide and urea.

Cardiac Output (CO) formula:

CO = Stroke Volume (SV) × Heart Rate (HR)

The Respiratory System

The respiratory system is responsible for taking in oxygen and expelling carbon dioxide, a waste product of metabolism.

Pulmonary Ventilation formula:

Pulmonary Ventilation = Tidal Volume (TV) × Respiratory Rate (RR)

The Nervous System

The nervous system is a complex network of nerves and cells that transmit signals throughout the body. It is responsible for sensing the environment, interpreting information, and triggering responses.

Membrane Potential formula (Nernst Equation):

E = (RT / zF) * ln([ion]outside / [ion]inside)

The Digestive System

The digestive system is responsible for breaking down food into absorbable nutrients, which are then transported to cells via the bloodstream.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) formula:

BMR = 66.5 + (13.75 × weight in kg) + (5.003 × height in cm) - (6.75 × age in years)

The Musculoskeletal System

The musculoskeletal system allows movement, supports the body, and protects internal organs.

Muscle Force formula:

F = m × a

Biochemical Processes in Human Biology

Cellular Respiration

Cellular respiration is the process by which cells convert glucose into energy (ATP) in the presence of oxygen.

Cellular Respiration Equation:

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂ → 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O + 36-38 ATP

Protein Synthesis

Protein synthesis is the process by which cells build proteins, vital for structure and function.

Rate of Protein Synthesis formula:

Protein Synthesis Rate = (Amino Acids Incorporated) / (Time in hours)

Enzyme Activity

Enzymes are biological catalysts that accelerate chemical reactions.

Michaelis-Menten Kinetics formula:

v = (Vmax × [S]) / (Km + [S])

Punnett Square

DNA Replication

DNA replication is essential for cell division. It ensures that each daughter cell gets a complete copy of the genome.

Rate of DNA Replication formula:

Rate of DNA Synthesis = (Number of Nucleotides Added) / (Time in seconds)

Homeostasis

Thermoregulation

The body maintains a constant temperature of around 37°C (98.6°F).

Heat Loss formula:

Q = m × c × ΔT
