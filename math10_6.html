Solid Figures Overview

Solid Figures

Solid figures, also known as 3D shapes or three-dimensional objects, have depth, width, and height. Unlike 2D shapes, which only have length and width, solid figures exist in a three-dimensional space. These shapes are often referred to as "solids" because they have volume. Solid figures include basic shapes such as cubes, cylinders, spheres, cones, pyramids, and prisms.

Types of Solid Figures and Their Properties:

1. Cube

A cube is a 3D figure with six equal square faces. All angles are right angles, and all edges are of equal length.

Volume:
V = a³
Surface Area:
A = 6a²

2. Cuboid (Rectangular Prism)

A cuboid is a 3D shape with six rectangular faces. Opposite faces are congruent.

Volume:
V = l × w × h
Surface Area:
A = 2(lw + lh + wh)

3. Sphere

A sphere is a perfectly round 3D figure, where every point on its surface is equidistant from its center.

Volume:
V = (4/3)πr³
Surface Area:
A = 4πr²

4. Cylinder

A cylinder consists of two circular bases connected by a curved surface.

Volume:
V = πr²h
Surface Area:
A = 2πr(h + r)

5. Cone

A cone has a circular base and a single curved surface that tapers to a point (the apex).

Volume:
V = (1/3)πr²h
Surface Area:
A = πr(r + l)

6. Pyramid

A pyramid has a polygonal base and triangular faces that meet at a single point (the apex).

Volume:
V = (1/3) × Base Area × Height
Surface Area:
A = Base Area + (1/2) × Perimeter × Slant Height

7. Prism

A prism is a solid with two parallel, congruent polygonal bases. The sides are parallelograms.

Volume:
V = Base Area × Height
Surface Area:
A = 2 × Base Area + Perimeter × Height

Important Concepts Related to Solid Figures

  • Surface Area: The total area that the surface of a solid occupies. It is the sum of the areas of all the faces of a solid.
  • Volume: The amount of space occupied by a solid object. It's expressed in cubic units (e.g., cubic meters, cubic centimeters).
  • Slant Height: For cones and pyramids, the slant height is the distance from the apex to the edge of the base, measured along the lateral surface. It’s used in the surface area formula for cones and pyramids.
  • Lateral Surface Area: The lateral surface area of a solid refers to the area of the faces excluding the base(s).

Summary of Formulas:

  • Cube: Volume = a³, Surface Area = 6a²
  • Cuboid: Volume = l × w × h, Surface Area = 2(lw + lh + wh)
  • Sphere: Volume = (4/3)πr³, Surface Area = 4πr²
  • Cylinder: Volume = πr²h, Surface Area = 2πr(h + r)
  • Cone: Volume = (1/3)πr²h, Surface Area = πr(r + l)
  • Pyramid: Volume = (1/3) × Base Area × Height, Surface Area = Base Area + (1/2) × Perimeter × Slant Height
  • Prism: Volume = Base Area × Height, Surface Area = 2 × Base Area + Perimeter × Height

Example Problems:

1. Cube Example:

If a cube has an edge length of 4 cm, find its volume and surface area:

Volume: V = a³ = 4³ = 64 cm³

Surface Area: A = 6a² = 6 × 4² = 96 cm²

2. Cylinder Example:

If a cylinder has a radius of 5 cm and a height of 10 cm, find its volume and surface area:

Volume: V = πr²h = π × 5² × 10 = 250π ≈ 785.4 cm³

Surface Area: A = 2πr(h + r) = 2π × 5(10 + 5) = 150π ≈ 471.2 cm²
