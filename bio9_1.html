Introduction to Biology: The Study of Life

What is Biology?

Biology is the scientific study of life and living organisms, covering structure, function, growth, origin, evolution, and interactions with the environment.

Characteristics of Living Organisms

  • Cellular Organization
  • Metabolism
  • Homeostasis
  • Growth and Development
  • Reproduction
  • Response to Stimuli
  • Adaptation and Evolution

Branches of Biology

  • Zoology - Study of Animals
  • Botany - Study of Plants
  • Microbiology - Study of Microorganisms
  • Genetics - Study of Heredity
  • Ecology - Study of Organisms & Environment
  • Physiology - Study of Body Functions

The Cell: Basic Unit of Life

Cells are the fundamental units of life, classified into Prokaryotic (bacteria) and Eukaryotic (human, plant, animal) cells.

Biochemistry: The Chemical Basis of Life

Living organisms are made of key biomolecules:

  • Carbohydrates - Energy source
  • Proteins - Building and repairing tissues
  • Lipids - Energy storage
  • Nucleic Acids (DNA, RNA) - Genetic information

Metabolism: Energy Flow

  • Photosynthesis: Plants convert sunlight into energy.
  • Cellular Respiration: Organisms break down glucose to release energy.

Genetics: Blueprint of Life

Genetics studies how traits are inherited. DNA, made of genes, carries hereditary information.

Evolution & Natural Selection

Charles Darwin's theory explains how organisms evolve to survive better in their environment.

Ecology: Life & the Environment

Ecology studies interactions among organisms and their surroundings.

Human Body Systems

  • Circulatory - Pumps blood
  • Respiratory - Oxygen intake
  • Digestive - Breaks down food
  • Nervous - Controls body functions
  • Immune - Defends against diseases
