Motion in a Straight Line

Key Concepts of Motion in a Straight Line:

  • Position (x): The location of an object along a straight path, typically represented by the symbol x.
  • Displacement (s): The shortest distance between the initial and final positions of the object in a straight line, along with the direction.
  • Distance (d): The total path length covered by an object, irrespective of direction.
  • Velocity (v): The rate of change of displacement with respect to time. It is a vector quantity.
  • Speed (s): The rate of change of distance with respect to time. It is a scalar quantity.
  • Acceleration (a): The rate of change of velocity with respect to time. It is a vector quantity.

Equations of Motion for Uniformly Accelerated Motion:

  • First Equation of Motion:
    v = u + at
    Where:
    • v is the final velocity
    • u is the initial velocity
    • a is the acceleration
    • t is the time
  • Second Equation of Motion:
    s = ut + ½at²
    Where:
    • s is the displacement
    • u is the initial velocity
    • a is the acceleration
    • t is the time
  • Third Equation of Motion:
    v² = u² + 2as
    Where:
    • v is the final velocity
    • u is the initial velocity
    • a is the acceleration
    • s is the displacement

Example Problems:

Problem 1:

A car starts from rest and accelerates at a rate of 2 m/s² for 5 seconds. Find the final velocity and the displacement of the car.

Solution:

  • u = 0 m/s (starts from rest)
  • a = 2 m/s²
  • t = 5 seconds

Using the first equation of motion: v = u + at = 0 + 2 × 5 = 10 m/s

Using the second equation of motion: s = ut + ½at² = 0 + ½ × 2 × 5² = 25 m

Therefore, the final velocity is 10 m/s and the displacement is 25 m.

Problem 2:

A ball is dropped from a height of 20 meters. Find the time it takes for the ball to reach the ground (assuming acceleration due to gravity g = 9.8 m/s²).

Solution:

  • u = 0 m/s (dropped from rest)
  • s = 20 m
  • g = 9.8 m/s²

Using the second equation of motion: s = ut + ½gt²
20 = 0 + ½ × 9.8 × t²
20 = 4.9t²
t² = 20 / 4.9 ≈ 4.08
t ≈ √4.08 ≈ 2.02 seconds

Therefore, it takes approximately 2.02 seconds for the ball to hit the ground.
