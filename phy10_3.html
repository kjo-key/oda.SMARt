Elasticity and Static Equilibrium of Rigid Bodies

I. Elasticity

Elasticity is the property of a material that allows it to return to its original shape after being deformed by an external force.

1. Stress and Strain

(a) Stress (σ)

Stress is the force applied per unit area of a material.

σ = F / A

(b) Strain (ε)

Strain is the relative deformation of a material due to applied stress.

ε = ΔL / L₀

2. Hooke’s Law

For small deformations, stress is directly proportional to strain.

σ = E ε

3. Moduli of Elasticity

1. Young’s Modulus: E = (σ / ε) = (F L₀) / (A ΔL)

2. Shear Modulus: G = (Shear Stress) / (Shear Strain)

3. Bulk Modulus: B = - (ΔP / (ΔV / V₀))

4. Poisson’s Ratio (ν)

ν = - (ε_lateral / ε_longitudinal)

5. Elastic Potential Energy

U = (1/2) E ε² V

II. Static Equilibrium of Rigid Bodies

1. Conditions for Equilibrium

1. Translational Equilibrium: ∑F = 0

2. Rotational Equilibrium: ∑τ = 0

2. Torque (Moment of Force)

τ = r F sinθ

3. Center of Mass and Center of Gravity

Discrete: x_cm = (∑mᵢxᵢ) / ∑mᵢ

Continuous: x_cm = (∫x dm) / ∫dm

4. Equilibrium of a Rigid Body

Force balance: ∑F_x = 0, ∑F_y = 0

Torque balance: ∑τ = 0

5. Types of Equilibrium

  • Stable Equilibrium
  • Unstable Equilibrium
  • Neutral Equilibrium

6. Common Applications

  • Lever and Fulcrum: F₁ d₁ = F₂ d₂
  • Ladders and Beams
  • Bridges and Structures
