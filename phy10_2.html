Uniformly Accelerated Motion

Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM)

Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM) refers to the motion of an object where its acceleration remains constant over time. This means the velocity of the object changes at a uniform rate.

Key Variables in UAM

  • Displacement (s): The change in position of an object.
  • Initial Velocity (u): The velocity of the object at the start.
  • Final Velocity (v): The velocity of the object after time t.
  • Acceleration (a): The rate of change of velocity.
  • Time (t): The duration for which motion occurs.

Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

1st Equation: Velocity-Time Relation

v = u + at

2nd Equation: Displacement-Time Relation

s = ut + (1/2) a t²

3rd Equation: Velocity-Displacement Relation

v² = u² + 2as

4th Equation: Average Velocity

v_avg = (u + v) / 2

Graphical Representation

  • Velocity-Time Graph: A straight line with a slope equal to acceleration.
  • Displacement-Time Graph: A parabolic curve for uniformly accelerated motion.

Special Cases in UAM

1. Free Fall Motion

Acceleration due to gravity: g = 9.8 m/s² (downward)

v = u + gt
s = ut + (1/2) gt²
v² = u² + 2gs

2. Motion on an Inclined Plane

Acceleration along the plane: a = g sin(θ)

Derivations of Equations

1st Equation: v = u + at

From acceleration definition:

a = (v - u) / t

Rearranging gives:

v = u + at

2nd Equation: s = ut + (1/2) at²

Velocity is the rate of change of displacement:

ds = v dt

Substituting v = u + at:

ds = (u + at) dt

Integrating:

s = ut + (1/2) at²

3rd Equation: v² = u² + 2as

Using v = u + at and solving for t, substituting into displacement formula:

v² = u² + 2as

Applications of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

  • Projectile Motion – Objects launched at an angle follow UAM in the vertical direction.
  • Car Braking Systems – Deceleration calculations use UAM equations.
  • Roller Coasters – The motion of carts follows UAM principles.
