Physics Concepts: Force, Work, Energy, Power

1. Force

Definition: Force is any interaction that, when unopposed, will change the motion of an object. It is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction.

Units: The SI unit of force is the Newton (N). 1 N is the force required to accelerate a 1 kg mass by 1 meter per second squared.

Formula:

F = ma

Where:

  • F is the force in Newtons (N)
  • m is the mass in kilograms (kg)
  • a is the acceleration in meters per second squared (m/s²)

2. Work

Definition: Work is done when a force is applied to an object, causing it to move in the direction of the force. Work is a scalar quantity, meaning it only has magnitude.

Units: The SI unit of work is the Joule (J), where 1 Joule is the work done when a force of 1 Newton is applied to move an object by 1 meter.

Formula:

W = F . d . cos(θ)

Where:

  • W is the work done in Joules (J)
  • F is the applied force in Newtons (N)
  • d is the displacement in meters (m)
  • θ is the angle between the force and displacement vector

Special Cases:

  • If the force is applied in the same direction as the displacement (θ = 0°): 
    W = F . d
  • If the force is applied perpendicular to the displacement (θ = 90°): 
    W = 0

3. Energy

Definition: Energy is the capacity to do work. It can exist in various forms, such as kinetic energy, potential energy, thermal energy, etc.

Types of Energy:

  • Kinetic Energy (KE): The energy possessed by an object due to its motion. Formula: 
    KE = 1/2 mv²
  • Potential Energy (PE): The energy stored in an object due to its position or configuration. Formula (gravitational PE near Earth's surface): 
    PE = mgh
  • Elastic Potential Energy: Stored energy in objects that can be stretched or compressed, like springs. Formula: 
    PE = 1/2 kx²

4. Power

Definition: Power is the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. It measures how quickly energy is used or transferred.

Units: The SI unit of power is the Watt (W), where 1 Watt is 1 Joule of work done per second.

Formula:

P = W / t

Where:

  • P is the power in Watts (W)
  • W is the work done in Joules (J)
  • t is the time in seconds (s)

Power in terms of force and velocity:

P = F . v

Where:

  • P is the power in Watts (W)
  • F is the force in Newtons (N)
  • v is the velocity in meters per second (m/s)
