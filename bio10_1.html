Sub-fields of Biology

1. Molecular Biology

Molecular biology focuses on the structure and function of the molecules that make up living organisms, particularly DNA, RNA, and proteins. It investigates how these molecules interact to drive cellular processes such as replication, transcription, and translation.

Key Concepts:

  • DNA Replication: The process by which a cell duplicates its DNA.
  • Transcription: The process of creating RNA from DNA.
  • Translation: The process of synthesizing proteins based on the RNA template.
  • Enzyme Kinetics: Describes the rate of enzyme-catalyzed reactions.

Important Formulas:

Michaelis-Menten Equation (enzyme kinetics):
V = (Vmax[S]) / (Km + [S])
DNA Quantification (Absorbance method):
DNA concentration = (A260 × dilution factor × 50) / 1

2. Genetics

Genetics is the study of heredity and variation in organisms. It examines how traits are passed from one generation to the next through genes and how those traits can be inherited, expressed, or modified.

Key Concepts:

  • Mendelian Genetics: Gregor Mendel's laws of inheritance (dominant/recessive traits, law of segregation, law of independent assortment).
  • Genotype and Phenotype: Genotype refers to the genetic makeup, while phenotype is the physical expression of genes.
  • Punnett Squares: A tool used to predict the probability of offspring inheriting particular alleles.

Important Formulas:

Hardy-Weinberg Equation (Population Genetics):
p2 + 2pq + q2 = 1

3. Cell Biology

Cell biology focuses on the structure and function of cells, the basic unit of life. This sub-field studies how cells interact with their environment and how they replicate, divide, and carry out essential life processes.

Key Concepts:

  • Cell Cycle: The series of events that lead to cell division.
  • Cellular Respiration: The process of converting glucose into energy (ATP).
  • Mitosis and Meiosis: The two types of cell division that produce somatic and gamete cells, respectively.

Important Formulas:

Cellular Respiration Efficiency:
Efficiency = (Energy used for work) / (Total energy released)

4. Ecology

Ecology is the study of ecosystems and how organisms interact with each other and their environment. It investigates populations, communities, and the biotic and abiotic factors that influence life.

Key Concepts:

  • Food Chains and Webs: Describes the flow of energy through ecosystems.
  • Biomes: Large-scale ecosystems defined by climate, soil type, and vegetation.
  • Carrying Capacity: The maximum population size an environment can support.

Important Formulas:

Exponential Growth Equation:
N(t) = N0 ert
Logistic Growth Equation:
N(t) = (K N0) / (N0 + (K - N0) e-rt)

5. Physiology

Physiology is the study of how living organisms function. It examines how organs, tissues, and cells work together to maintain homeostasis.

Key Concepts:

  • Homeostasis: The maintenance of a stable internal environment.
  • Circulatory System: How blood flows through the heart, arteries, veins, and capillaries.
  • Respiratory System: How organisms exchange gases (oxygen and carbon dioxide).

Important Formulas:

Cardiac Output (for circulatory system):
CO = SV × HR
Ventilation Rate:
V = T × f

6. Evolutionary Biology

Evolutionary biology investigates the processes that have led to the diversity of life on Earth. It includes the study of natural selection, genetic drift, mutation, and speciation.

Key Concepts:

  • Natural Selection: The process by which traits that increase an organism's fitness become more common in a population.
  • Adaptation: The process by which species evolve traits that enhance survival and reproduction.
  • Speciation: The formation of new and distinct species in the course of evolution.

Important Formulas:

Fitness of a Trait:
W = (Number of offspring with trait) / (Total number of offspring)
Genetic Drift (Change in Allele Frequency):
Δp = p(1 - p) × 1 / (2N)

7. Botany

Botany is the branch of biology that deals with the study of plants, including their structure, growth, reproduction, metabolism, and diseases.

Key Concepts:

  • Photosynthesis: The process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy.
  • Plant Growth and Development: How plants grow and develop from seed to mature organism.
  • Plant Reproduction: Sexual and asexual reproduction in plants.

Important Formulas:

Photosynthesis Equation:
6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy → C6H12O6 + 6O2
Rate of Photosynthesis:
R = (ΔOxygen produced) / (ΔTime)
