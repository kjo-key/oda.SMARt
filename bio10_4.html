Cell Reproduction

Mitosis

Stages of Mitosis

Mitosis is divided into several stages, each with distinct processes.

  • Interphase: Preparation for mitosis (G1, S, and G2 phases).
  • Prophase: Chromatin condenses into chromosomes, spindle fibers form.
  • Metaphase: Chromosomes align at the center of the cell.
  • Anaphase: Sister chromatids are pulled toward opposite poles.
  • Telophase: New nuclear membranes form around the separated chromosomes.
  • Cytokinesis: The cell divides into two daughter cells.

Formula for Chromosome Number in Mitosis:

2n → 2n

Where 2n is the diploid number of chromosomes in the parent cell. After mitosis, the daughter cells also have 2n chromosomes.

Meiosis

Stages of Meiosis

Meiosis involves two rounds of division: Meiosis I and Meiosis II.

  • Prophase I: Homologous chromosomes pair up and cross over.
  • Metaphase I: Homologous chromosomes align at the center.
  • Anaphase I: Homologous chromosomes are pulled to opposite poles.
  • Telophase I: Two daughter cells are formed, each with half the chromosome number (haploid).
  • Meiosis II: Similar to mitosis, but it involves the division of haploid cells into four non-identical haploid cells.

Formula for Chromosome Number in Meiosis:

2n → n

In Meiosis I, the chromosome number is halved. The diploid parent cell (2n) produces two haploid cells (n). After Meiosis II, the number remains n, resulting in four haploid daughter cells.

Key Formulas Related to Cell Reproduction

  • Cell Cycle Duration: T = TG1 + TS + TG2 + TM
  • Chromosome Number in Mitosis: 2n → 2n
  • Chromosome Number in Meiosis: 2n → n
  • Crossing Over and Genetic Recombination: Possible Genetic Variants = 2n
  • Genetic Variation after Meiosis: Number of Genetic Combinations = 2n

Mendel's Laws of Inheritance

  • Law of Segregation: The two alleles for a trait separate during gamete formation.
  • Law of Independent Assortment: Genes located on different chromosomes assort independently of each other.
