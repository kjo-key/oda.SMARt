Statistics and Probability

1. Statistics

Statistics is the study of data collection, organization, analysis, interpretation, and presentation.

1.1 Types of Statistics

  • Descriptive Statistics – Summarizes data (e.g., mean, median, mode).
  • Inferential Statistics – Makes predictions using samples.

1.2 Measures of Central Tendency

  • Mean: (Sum of all values) ÷ (Number of values)
  • Median: The middle value when data is arranged in order.
  • Mode: The most frequently occurring value.
Note: The mean is sensitive to outliers, while the median is more resistant to extreme values.

1.3 Measures of Dispersion

  • Range: Maximum value - Minimum value
  • Variance: Average of squared differences from the mean.
  • Standard Deviation: Square root of variance.

2. Probability

Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to occur, expressed as a number between 0 and 1.

2.1 Probability Rules

  • Addition Rule: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
  • Multiplication Rule: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B), if A and B are independent
  • Conditional Probability: P(A given B) = P(A and B) ÷ P(B)
Example: If rolling a die, the probability of getting a 3 is 1/6.

2.2 Bayes' Theorem

P(A given B) = [P(B given A) × P(A)] ÷ P(B)

2.3 Probability Distributions

  • Binomial Distribution: Probability of exactly k successes in n trials.
  • Poisson Distribution: Probability of a given number of events in a fixed interval.
  • Normal Distribution: Bell-shaped curve describing many natural events.
Tip: The normal distribution is used in standardized tests like IQ scores.
